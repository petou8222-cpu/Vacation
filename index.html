Why I Love It

This island is my reset button. The water is turquoise, nights are quiet, and phone notifications finally stop feeling important. There’s a simple rhythm: swim, nap, eat fresh seafood, repeat. Sunsets stretch across the bay and bioluminescent plankton sparkle at night.

  • Calm beaches (Saracen Bay, Sunset Beach)
  • Affordable guesthouses + bungalows
  • Bioluminescent plankton after dark
  • Easy boat access from Sihanoukville

Top Things To Do

Swim & Snorkel

Shallow, clear water with coral and small fish. Go in the morning for glassy conditions.

Sunset Hike

Walk the short jungle path to Sunset Beach. Bring a flashlight for the way back.

Night Plankton

On moonless nights, tiny plankton glow when you move the water—pure magic.

Café Crawl

Try a coconut shake by day and grilled seafood by night along Saracen Bay.

Three Favorite Memories

  1. First sunrise swim. The whole bay was quiet, just ripples and pink light.
  2. Bioluminescence. We turned off the lights and the ocean looked like stars.
  3. Stormy afternoon. Reading in a bungalow while rain drummed on the roof.

Travel Tips

  • Best time: Nov–Apr (dry season). Rainy season is beautiful, but expect showers.
  • How to get there: Fast ferry from Sihanoukville → Saracen Bay (30–45 mins).
  • Cash: Bring enough USD/KHR; ATMs are limited on the island.
  • Connectivity: Wi-Fi exists but can be slow—embrace the digital detox.
  • Respect: Pack out trash; reefs and wildlife are fragile.

Plan Your Trip (Quick Guide)

2-Day Sample Plan

  • Morning ferry → check in at Saracen Bay
  • Swim + lunch: mango salad & grilled squid
  • Hike to Sunset Beach → watch sunset
  • Night swim to see plankton (moonless night = best)
  • Day 2: snorkel tour or hammock + book day
